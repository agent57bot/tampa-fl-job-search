## Resume Formatting Best Practices

### ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Optimization
- **Save as:** .docx (most compatible) AND .pdf (for human review)
- **Font:** Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman (11-12pt)
- **Avoid:** Tables, graphics, headers/footers, text boxes (ATS can't read)
- **Use:** Standard section headings (Professional Summary, Experience, Education, Skills)
- **Keywords:** Mirror job posting language where truthful

### Length
- **Target:** 1 page (early career = less than 3 years experience)
- **Max:** 1.5 pages if including detailed project descriptions
- **Current resume:** Fits 1 page comfortably—keep it that way

### Quantification Strategy
Add numbers wherever possible:
- Project square footage (already doing this—good!)
- Budget (if known): "Designed MEP systems for $X million project"
- Bed count / capacity: "Expanded hospital from 100 to 218 beds"
- Team size: "Coordinated with 5-person multidisciplinary team"
- Timeline: "Delivered design on accelerated 6-month schedule"

**Ask current employer for permission to cite:**
- Project budgets
- Schedule performance
- Team size

### Action Verb Variety
**Current resume overuses:** "Designed," "Coordinated"  
**Add variety:**
- Analyzed, Calculated, Specified, Modeled, Collaborated, Managed, Delivered, Resolved, Maintained, Ensured, Performed, Supported

**Example transformation:**
- Before: "Designed fire protection system"
- After: "Specified NFPA 13 wet-pipe sprinkler system including fire pump, backflow preventer, and 200+ sprinkler heads"

## Cover Letter Framework

### Structure (3-4 paragraphs, ~300-400 words)

**Paragraph 1: Hook + Why This Company**
```
I am writing to apply for the [Job Title] position at [Company]. As a Tampa-based Mechanical Engineer with specialized healthcare MEP experience and a passion for [fire protection/sustainable design/complex systems], I am drawn to [Company]'s [specific project/value/reputation]. Your work on [specific project or client] aligns with my background in [relevant experience], and I am eager to contribute to [Company]'s mission of [research their mission statement].
```

**Paragraph 2: Evidence (The "Proof" Paragraph)**
```
In my current role at Affiliated Engineers, I have designed MEP systems for over [X SF] of healthcare facilities, including a 415,000 SF hospital campus for Sarasota Memorial Healthcare System. This project required [specific technical challenge, e.g., "coordinating fire protection risers through three-level interstitial spaces while maintaining compliance with NFPA 13 and FGI Guidelines"]. I successfully [quantified outcome, e.g., "delivered clash-free fire protection design on an accelerated schedule"], demonstrating my ability to [skill required by job posting]. Additionally, my work on pharmaceutical facilities has developed my expertise in [relevant technical area].
```

**Paragraph 3: Why You're Making This Move**
```
I am seeking to [grow my fire protection expertise / work on larger-scale projects / join a collaborative team culture / contribute to sustainable healthcare infrastructure] with an established firm like [Company]. While I value the experience gained at AEI, I am eager to [specific opportunity at new company, e.g., "work with a dedicated fire protection team" or "access training programs for PE development" or "contribute to [Company]'s nationally recognized healthcare portfolio"]. My PE exam preparation is underway, and I am committed to long-term growth in [specialty area].
```

**Paragraph 4: Close**
```
I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my healthcare MEP background and technical skills can contribute to [Company]'s projects. Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to speaking with you.
```

### Customization Checklist for Each Application
- [ ] Research company website for specific projects to mention
- [ ] Find hiring manager name (LinkedIn search, call company if needed)
- [ ] Mirror 2-3 keywords from job posting
- [ ] Identify company differentiator (ESOP culture, HCA partnership, global resources, etc.)
- [ ] Adjust tone: corporate (large firms) vs. entrepreneurial (small firms)
