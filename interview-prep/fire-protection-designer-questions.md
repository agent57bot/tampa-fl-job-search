# Fire Protection Designer Interview Prep – Complete Question Set

This comprehensive guide combines technical, behavioral, and fit questions for Fire Protection Designer interviews (e.g., BR+A, similar firms).

---

## **Part 1: Opening & Background**

### **1) Tell us about yourself.**

**Framework:** 30–60 seconds covering background → technical interests → why fire protection

**Key elements:**
- Engineering family background (mechanical + architecture exposure)
- Early technical curiosity (Hoffmans apparatus, solar modeling, etc.)
- Pivot to fire protection (when/why mechanical engineering → life safety)
- Current role summary at AEI

**Example structure:**  
*"I grew up in Tampa in an engineering family—father's a mechanical engineer, mother's an architect. I got interested in building systems early and studied Mechanical Engineering at USF. I moved into fire protection when I realized I wanted to work on systems that directly protect people. At AEI, I've been designing sprinkler and standpipe systems for commercial and institutional projects, which has given me solid code knowledge and hands-on coordination experience."*

**Use STAR format:**

### **Technical Design Questions**

### **6) Walk me through how you'd design a sprinkler system for a new warehouse.**

**Key steps to cover:**
1. Determine occupancy hazard classification
2. Calculate water demand (density × area of application)
3. Layout sprinkler spacing and coverage
4. Perform hydraulic calculations
5. Coordinate with architecture and other trades
6. Prepare permit submittal documents

### **7) How do you determine hazard classification, and why does it matter?**

**Cover:**
- NFPA 13 Occupancy Hazard vs Commodity Classification
- Light Hazard, Ordinary Hazard (Group 1/2), Extra Hazard (Group 1/2)
- Commodity classifications (Class I-IV, plastics)
- **Impact:** affects density, area of application, K-factor selection, piping requirements

### **8) How do you calculate required density/area and water demand?**

**Cover:**
- Density/area curves from NFPA 13
- Remote area concept (most hydraulically demanding area)
- Hose stream allowance
- Total GPM demand = sprinkler demand + hose stream

### **9) What factors do you use to select pipe schedule/material and layout?**

**Cover:**
- Schedule 10/40 vs CPVC vs other materials
- Tree system vs grid/loop
- Hydraulic calculation method (Hazen-Williams)
- Support and seismic bracing requirements

### **10) How do you approach standpipe design in a multi-story building?**

**Cover:**
- NFPA 14 requirements
- Class I (fire department use) vs Class II (occupant use) vs Class III (both)
- Wet vs dry vs automatic wet
- Pressure requirements at highest outlet
- Zone sizing for pressure control

### **11) Tell me about your process for hydraulic calculations.**

**Cover:**
- Software tools (HydraCALC, SprinkCAD, etc.)
- Remote area selection
- K-factor and discharge calculations
- Friction loss calculations
- Verification and QA checks

### **12) How do you coordinate sprinkler and fire alarm systems with other trades?**

**Cover:**
- Clash detection in BIM (Navisworks)
- Flow/tamper switch locations with electrical
- Structural coordination for hanger loads
- Architectural coordination for sprinkler head aesthetics
- Plumbing coordination for water supply
- HVAC coordination for duct/ceiling clearances

### **13) What do you check before finalizing shop drawings?**

**Cover:**
- Compliance with approved design documents
- Hydraulic calculations verification
- Sprinkler coverage verification
- Code compliance checklist
- Coordination with field conditions
- Material specifications
- Support and bracing details

## **Part 4: Code & Standards**

### **14) Which codes/standards do you use most often?**

**List key ones:**
- NFPA 13 (Sprinkler Systems)
- NFPA 14 (Standpipe and Hose Systems)
- NFPA 20 (Fire Pumps)
- NFPA 72 (Fire Alarm and Signaling)
- IFC (International Fire Code)
- IBC (International Building Code)
- Local amendments and AHJ requirements

### **15) How do you stay current on code changes?**

**Good answers:**
- NFPA membership and code cycle tracking
- Continuing education courses
- Industry webinars and conferences
- NICET study and certification maintenance
- Internal training programs
- Code update summaries from professional organizations

### **16) Can you describe a time local AHJ requirements differed from baseline NFPA guidance?**

**Use STAR format with specific example**

### **17) How do you handle conflicts between specs, code, and field conditions?**

**Cover:**
- Document review and hierarchy (code > specs > drawings typically)
- RFI process
- AHJ consultation when needed
- Engineering judgment documentation
- Change order procedures

### **18) What documentation do you prepare for permit submittals?**

**Cover:**
- Design drawings (plans, riser diagrams, details)
- Hydraulic calculations
- Product data sheets and certifications
- Design narrative/basis of design
- Code compliance matrix
- Fire pump curves and specifications

### **19) Tell us about a challenge and how you dealt with it.**

**Use STAR format**

**Best story types:**
- Caught major error before installation
- Solved tricky coordination issue
- Met impossible deadline
- Navigated AHJ pushback or code interpretation dispute

**What to include:**
- What the error was
- How you discovered it
- Impact if it had gone unnoticed
- How you corrected it
- Process improvements you implemented

### **21) How do you prioritize when multiple projects are due at once?**

**Cover:**
- Deadline assessment
- Stakeholder communication
- Task batching and efficiency
- Escalation when needed
- Time management tools/techniques

### **22) How do you handle disagreements with PMs, contractors, or inspectors?**

**Cover:**
- Active listening
- Code/technical basis for position
- Finding common ground
- Escalation path when needed
- Documentation of decisions

### **23) Tell me about a project that went wrong—what did you learn?**

**Be honest but constructive:**
- What happened
- Your role/responsibility
- What you learned
- How you've applied that lesson since
- Process/workflow improvements you made

## **Part 7: Questions for Them**

### **30) What questions do you have for us?**

**Strong questions to ask:**

**About the work:**
- What types of occupancies/projects are most common here?
- What's the typical project size and complexity range?
- How does BR+A approach innovation in fire protection (new tech, performance-based design)?
- Are there opportunities to work on specialized project types (healthcare, high-rise, industrial)?

**About the role:**
- How much design authority does this role have vs PM review/oversight?
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- What's the typical project team structure?
- How is work assigned and prioritized?

**About tools and development:**
- What software stack and standards library do you use?
- How do you support code training and continuing education?
- Do you support NICET certification and advancement?
- What professional development opportunities are available?

**About culture:**
- How would you describe the team culture and collaboration style?
- What do you enjoy most about working here?
- How does the firm support work-life balance?

**About growth:**
- What does the career path look like for this position?
- How does BR+A support professional registration (PE, NICET)?
- Are there opportunities for mentorship or specialty development?

## **Action Plan for Interview Prep**

### **Step 1: Write Your Answers**
Create detailed written answers for:
- "Tell us about yourself"
- "Why BR+A?" (research first)
- "Why leave AEI?"
- 2–3 project STAR stories (most impressive projects)
- 2–3 challenge/problem-solving STAR stories

### **Step 2: Practice Out Loud**
- Don't just read—speak answers conversationally
- Record yourself and listen back
- Time your responses (30-60 sec for intro, 2-3 min for STAR stories)
- Practice until answers feel natural, not memorized

### **Step 3: Mock Interview**
- Have someone ask questions randomly from this list
- Practice answering without notes
- Get feedback on clarity, confidence, technical accuracy
- Practice handling unexpected follow-up questions

### **Step 4: Research BR+A Thoroughly**
- Recent projects (portfolio, case studies, press releases)
- Leadership team (LinkedIn profiles)
- Company culture (Glassdoor reviews, LinkedIn posts)
- Fire protection community reputation
- Recent awards or recognition
- Office locations and project geography

### **Step 5: Prepare Your Questions**
- Select 5–7 questions from Part 7 that genuinely interest you
- Prioritize based on what you learn during the interview
- Have backup questions in case some are answered during the conversation

### **Step 6: Logistics**
- Confirm interview time, location, format (in-person/virtual)
- Test technology if virtual (Zoom, Teams, etc.)
- Prepare professional background for virtual interviews
- Bring copies of resume, portfolio, and references
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early (in-person) or log in 5 minutes early (virtual)
